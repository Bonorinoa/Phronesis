You · 02:14
Walk me through the Bellman equation. Why is the discount factor less than one?
Phronesis · 02:21
The Bellman equation recursively defines the value of capital as the maximum over current and future consumption. The discount factor β, less than one, makes present utility worth more than future utility — a mathematical encoding of time preference.
You · 02:38
And how does Sargent frame it differently?
Phronesis · 02:44
Sargent emphasizes the recursive competitive equilibrium framing — agents solve their own problem taking prices as given. The Bellman is the agent's problem; equilibrium prices are determined separately.
You · 02:51
So is the planner's problem a special case?
Listening Yes — when prices equal marginal rates of substitution. We'll go deeper on that next.
